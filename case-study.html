HypeXray

Turning hype into structure with a privacy-first, client-side tool.

Client-side web app · Live MVP · GitHub Pages

When evaluating online offers, whether courses, services, or travel experiences, the hardest part is rarely the price.

The real challenge is understanding what is actually being offered versus how it is framed.

The problem

Most offers blend deliverables, promises, urgency, and omissions into a single persuasive stream. Buyers are expected to decode structure while under emotional pressure.

I wanted a way to slow the decision down and separate concrete terms from marketing language, without labeling anything as good, bad, or fraudulent.

  • What do you actually get?
  • What is stated versus implied?
  • What information is missing?
  • What questions should be asked before deciding?

The idea

HypeXray is a small client-side tool that turns marketing copy into a calm, structured snapshot.

You paste an offer and the app breaks it into neutral boxes:

  • What’s being offered
  • Price signals
  • Timeline and access
  • Claims and promises
  • Pressure patterns
  • Missing information to ask about

Constraints and principles

  • Client-side only. No backend, no uploads.
  • Privacy-first by design.
  • Mobile-first and installable as an app.
  • Neutral tone. No fear-based framing.
  • Low cognitive load. Boxes over dashboards.

Implementation

  • Single index.html architecture
  • Vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • Hosted via GitHub Pages
  • PWA support using manifest.json
  • Optional privacy-respectful analytics

Outcome

HypeXray shipped as a live, installable MVP in a single day.

It now functions as:

  • a practical decision-support tool
  • a public demonstration of calm system design
  • a foundation for higher-touch clarity services

What this enabled next

While HypeXray works well as a self-serve tool, higher-stakes decisions often benefit from a human second pass.

That insight led directly to the creation of a follow-on service: Offer Clarity Pass.
